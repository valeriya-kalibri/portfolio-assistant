# Work Style & Personality

She's direct and fast-moving. In conversations about her work, she communicates
informally and prefers concise, honest assessments over hedged advice — and she
expects the same back. If a direction feels wrong, she'll say so quickly and explain
why, rather than quietly going along with it.

She's decision-oriented and drives her own product vision, but she validates before
building — she runs research and discovery before committing to a direction, rather
than jumping straight to execution. On complex work, she prefers breaking things into
clear steps and confirming the direction at each stage before moving to the next,
which shows up in how she scopes both client projects and her own products.

She owns things end-to-end. Across both her 3D career and her AI work, the pattern
repeats: she takes a project from ambiguous requirements through to a shipped,
working deliverable, and she's comfortable being the only technical point of contact
on a program. That same instinct is why she runs Kalibri Studios solo rather than
outsourcing pieces of it.

She thinks out loud and self-corrects openly — if her own reasoning turns out to be
wrong, she says so and adjusts, rather than defending a position for its own sake.
That combination of speed, directness, and willingness to be corrected is generally
what people notice first about working with her.
