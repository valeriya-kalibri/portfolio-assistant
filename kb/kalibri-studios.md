# Kalibri Studios

Kalibri Studios is Valeriya's own AI consulting and automation company, based in
Orlando and structured as a single-member LLC. She founded and runs it solo — there's
no team to hand off to, so she owns technical architecture, client-facing scoping,
and sales all at once. It's her second time building this company: she originally
founded Kalibri Studios in 2018 as a commercial 3D visualization studio, paused it to
take roles at DiSTI and HII, then relaunched it in January 2026 as an AI consulting
and automation company, initially alongside her HII role before going full-time on it
in March 2026.

The company targets small and mid-sized businesses — roughly $500K–$5M in annual
revenue — that want AI automation but don't have an internal team to build it. Her
product portfolio, in priority order:

1. **Kalibri AI Analytics** — a business intelligence platform, and the flagship
   product. Built for operators who need consolidated, cross-location analytics
   without hiring a data team.
2. **AI Front Desk Voice Agent** — a voice-based agent that handles live customer
   calls end-to-end: answering, qualifying, and booking.
3. **AI Lead Capture Chatbot** — a conversational agent that qualifies inbound leads
   and hands off structured contact data for follow-up.

Beyond that client-facing portfolio, she's also built two internal/exploratory
projects. **LeadPulse** is an internal prospect-qualification tool that runs Kalibri
Studios' own sales pipeline — not a client-facing product; she built it to prove the
scoring logic on her own outreach before considering a multi-tenant SaaS version.
**Kalibri Sidekick** is a real-time call coaching assistant — built and deployed, but
still pre-go-to-market, with self-serve SaaS versus direct B2B sales undecided pending
usage data.

Her primary validated vertical is medical aesthetics — med spas, particularly
multi-location operators who need consolidated reporting across locations. She's
deliberate about not overclaiming that niche publicly before it's proven with paying
clients; the strategy is direct sales to individual operators first, building
credibility, before any conversation about platform or franchise-level partnerships.
